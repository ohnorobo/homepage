Interesting use case for google translate

A while ago I came upon a quote

Without authority there could not be worse violence than that of authority under existing conditions

Which I knew was translated from some slavic language, and I wanted to find the original source. Basic googling comes up with an essay called On Anarchy, from Tolstoy’s Government is Violence. But there’s no source online, and I wouldn’t know what to do with the original Russian anyway.

It also gives a google bookwhich uses the quote and gives a fuller context

The anarchists are right in everything; in the negation of the existing order, and in the assertion that, without authority there could be no worse violence than that of authority under existing conditions. They are mistaken only in thinking that anarchy can be instituted by a revolution.

Okay fine, but no amount of googling in English is going to get us a Russian source.

When I was first trying to find this about a year ago I hit a dead end here, but google translate has improved enough over time to be useful.

First pass with our English gives us

Без власти не может быть хуже, чем насилие, что власти при существующих условиях

But the internet doesn’t knowthat phrase, so obviously machine translation has failed us again.

But google gives alternate translations, so we just have to sort through them

Starting with

без разрешения

This is supposedly “without authority” but googling itand translating the results back into English gives us mostly instances of “without a permit,” clearly wrong.

So instead we’ll try the google alternate

без власти

Which in contexttranslates back to the much more reasonable ”without power”

Moving through a few distinctive phrases like this we can pick more accurate translations manually, and when we google those wordswe get this pagecontaining

Анархисты правы во всем: и в отрицании существующего и в утверждении того, что при существующих нравах ничего не может быть хуже насилия власти; но они грубо ошибаются, думая, что анархию можно установить революцией. Анархия может быть установлена только тем, что будет больше и больше людей, которым не нужна защита правительственной власти и все больше и больше людей, которые будут стыдиться прилагать эту власть.

Which (aha!) translates to

Anarchists are right in everything: in the denial of an existing approval and the fact that under current mores nothing can be worse than the abuse of power, but they are grossly mistaken in thinking that anarchy is possible to set a revolution. Anarchy can only be installed by the fact that there will be more and more people who do not need the protection of government and more and more people who would be ashamed to make that power.

Notice the translation of the actual phrase is totally different, but the surrounding context comes through clear enough.

Putting the new Russian into google translate lets us check the rough correspondence between words, and so find the boundaries of the phrase, which is

что при существующих нравах ничего не может быть хуже насилия власти

And now google loves us.
